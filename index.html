R.A. 9344, officially known as the Juvenile Justice and Welfare Act of 2006, is a law in the Philippines that aims to create a comprehensive system for dealing with children at risk and children in conflict with the law. It focuses on rehabilitation and reintegration rather than criminal prosecution.

R.A. 9344 represents a significant shift in the Philippines' approach to juvenile delinquency, emphasizing child welfare and rehabilitation over punishment. However, ongoing efforts are needed to ensure its effective implementation and adequate resources for supporting children in need.
